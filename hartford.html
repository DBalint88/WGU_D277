Hartford, CT

Hartford, CT is the capitol city of Connecticut. Located smack in the center of the state in Hartford County, and incorporated in 1637 the city is an urban district with a population of 120k people and an emphasis on Insurance. It is locally known as "The Insurance Capital of the World." The average income of a resident in Hartford is $37k/year, much lower than the state average of $84k/year.

Some attractions in Hartford, CT include The Mark Twain House, the Harriet Beecher Stowe center, and the Wadswrorth Athenium. There is also a Science Center for kids and a Civic Center for the whole family which frequently hosts a variety of different conventions, sports games, and concerts. There is also an active nightlife in the area with a great variety of pubs and restaurants. Anyone interested in visiting these tourist hot spots can fly in conveniently through Bradley International Airport located on the city's edge.

Major highways near Hartford include 84 and 91. The skirts right around the city and heads towards Danbury, CT to the west and the University of Connecticut in the Easy. I-91 runs straight through the city, and connects New Haven in the South to Springfield Massachusetts in the North.

None of these are particularly nice places to visit. New Haven is probably the best. Most folks in Connecticut spend time sparingly in their home state when they aren't actually in their own home. It's not that entertainment is altogether absent in the state, but it's mostly just overshadowed by the cultural giants of New York City and Boston.

The Hartford Courant is the nation's oldest running news paper.
