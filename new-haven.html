New Haven, CT

New Haven, CT is the second-largest city in Connecticut by population. Located midway down the southern coast of Connecticut along Long Island Sound, and incorporated in 1784, the city is an urban district with a population of 134k people and an emphasis on the arts. It is known for Yale University and as home to the best pizza in the world. The average income of a resident in New Haven is $49k/year, much lower than the state average of $84k/year.

Some attractions in New Haven, CT include Yale University, the Peabody Museum of Natural History, and the Yale Art Gallery. It also features a suprising number of outdoor recreation areas, including the beautiful East Rock state park. The aforementioned park is home to Judge's Cave, where two British judges were protected in exile after they sentenced their king to death. There is also an active nightlife in the area with a great variety of pubs and restaurants. Anyone interested in visiting these tourist hot spots can fly in through either Bradley International Airport to the north or JFK / Laguardia in NYC to the south.

Major highways near New Haven include 91 and 95. I-91 originates in the city and runs North through Hartford. I-95 runs along the connecticut coast, connecting Rhode Island in the East to New York and beyond in the south.

Did you know?

New Haven is the first planned city in America build using a grid system.
