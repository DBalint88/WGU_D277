Middletown, CT

Middletown, CT is a small city located in central CT only 20 minutes south of the state capital of Hartford. It was incorporated in 1651. The city is a suburban district with a small downtown shopping center. It has a population of 47k people and an emphasis on historical buildings. It is known for Qunnipiac University and Wadsworth State Falls. The average income of a resident in Middletown is $67k/year, substantially lower than the state average of $84k/year.

Some attractions in Middletown, CT include Quinnipiac University, the time-captured classic Roller Rink, and Wadsworth State Falls. It also features a beautiful view of the Connecticut river which runs along its Eastern border. It is also home to O'Rourkes Diner, famous for their delicious breakfast foods as well as Amato's Toy Store, a rare and comprehensive independent toy store. There is also an active nightlife in the area with a great variety of pubs and restaurants. Anyone interested in visiting these tourist hot spots can fly in through either Bradley International Airport to the north.

Major highways near Middletown include 91 and 9. 91 runs north to Hartford and South to New Haven. Route 9 runs North to New Britain and Farmington and South to Old Saybrook on the coast.

Did you know?

Middletown was incorporated as a town under its original Indian name, Mattabeseck.
